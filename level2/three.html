THE CHIMNEY SWEEPER

                                                      BY WILLIAM BLAKE


   When my mother died              I was   very young,
        And my father sold me              while yet my tongue
                                                         Could scarcely cry
                                             " 'weep!
                                                 'weep!
                                                  'weep!
                                                     'weep!"
  So your chimneys I sweep
                                                     & in soot       I sleep.


  There's little Tom Dacre,              who cried when his head
  That curled like a lamb's back, was shaved,
                                                                       so I said,
  "Hush, Tom!              never mind it,
                                   for when your head's bare ,
  You know that the soot cannot spoil your white hair."


  And so he was quiet,
                                   & that very night,
  As Tom was a-sleeping he had such a sight!
  That thousands of sweepers,
                                                Dick, Joe, Ned, & Jack,
  Were all of them locked up in   coffins of black;


  And by came an Angel
                                       who had a bright key,
  And he opened the coffins & set them all free;
  Then down    a   green    plain,
                                          leaping,
                                                    laughing they run,
  And wash     in a river      and shine in the Sun.


  Then naked & white,       all their bags left behind,
  They rise upon clouds, and sport in the wind.
       And the Angel    told Tom,
                                       if he'd be a good boy ,
  He'd have      God
                                  for his father       & never want joy .


  And so Tom awoke;
                                  and we rose in the dark
  And got with our bags & our brushes to work.
  Though the morning was cold, Tom was happy & warm;
  So if all do their duty,       they need not fear harm.
