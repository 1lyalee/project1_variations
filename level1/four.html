THE CHIMNEY SWEEPER

                                                                                          BY WILLIAM BLAKE


           When my mother died    I was   very young,                                                There's little Tom Dacre,     who cried when his head
        And my father sold me       while yet my tongue                                 That curled like a lamb's back, was shaved,
                                                         Could scarcely cry                          so I said,      "Hush, Tom!              never mind it,
                                             " 'weep! 'weep! 'weep! 'weep!"                       for when your head's bare,   You know that the soot cannot spoil your white hair."
                                     So your chimneys I sweep
                                                     & in soot      I sleep.


                     And so he was quiet,       & that very night,                                           And by came an Angel       who had a bright key,
                  As Tom was a-sleeping he had such a sight!                                  And he opened the coffins & set them all free;
           That thousands of sweepers,         Dick, Joe, Ned, & Jack,              Then down    a   green    plain,   leaping,   laughing they run,
                 Were all of them locked up in                                                            And wash     in a river      
                           coffins of black;                                                                             and shine in the Sun.


                                                                                                                                                                     Then
                                                                                                                                                                           naked
                                                                                                                                                                     & white,
                                                                                                                                          all their bags left behind,
                                                                                                                                          They rise upon clouds,
                                                                                                                                          and sport in the wind.
                                                                                                                                          And the Angel told Tom,
                                                                                                                                          if he'd be a good boy,
                                                                                                                                          He'd have God for his father
                                                                                                                                          & never want joy.
                                                                                                                                          And so Tom awoke;
                                                                                                                                          and we rose in the dark
                                                                                                                                          And got with our bags
                                                                                                                                          & our brushes to work.
                                                                                                                                          Though the morning was cold,
                                                                                                                                          Tom was happy & warm;
                                                                                                                                          So if all do their duty,
                                                                                                                                          they need not fear harm.
